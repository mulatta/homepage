I'm **Seungwon Lee**, an M.S. candidate at the
[SBEE Lab](https://sjanglab.org) (Synthetic Biology and Evolutionary
Bioengineering) at Incheon National University, advised by Sungho
Jang, Ph.D. My undergraduate training was in Bioengineering with a
minor in Computer Science, at the same school.

I work between two modes. On one side: genome-editing tool
development and ncRNA-based regulation. On the other: AI-driven
protein and RNA engineering, structure prediction, and NGS analysis
pipelines. Neither alone is enough, so I spend time on the bridge.

## Research interests

- **Synthetic Biology** — genome editing tool development (prime
  editing, base editing), ncRNA-based gene regulation.
- **Computational Biology** — AI-driven protein and RNA engineering,
  biomolecular structure prediction, NGS data analysis pipeline
  development.

## Currently

- **Inducible RNA regulators via RNA Capture-SELEX** — selecting
  aptamers against small molecules (buspirone, pioglitazone), from
  library design through SELEX rounds to NGS-based candidate
  analysis (fastp, Nextflow, AptaTrace, NUPACK).
- **Directed evolution with EvolvePro** — PLM-based active learning
  for activity optimization of the BenM and TphR transcription
  factors.

## How I work

- **Self-host when possible.** Minimize vendor lock-in, understand
  dependencies, keep cost predictable.
- **Declare systems, don't configure them.** If a machine dies I
  want to rebuild it in one command.
- **Fix root causes.** Suppressing a warning is a failure mode.
